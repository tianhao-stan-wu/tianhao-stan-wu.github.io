Something random that I found interesting:

1. [Occam's razor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occam%27s_razor), also known as the principle of parsimony, says "Entities must not be multiplied beyond necessity". Using Occam's razor, you can have a really short hair-cut, if not bald.

2. [God's number](https://web.williams.edu/Mathematics/sjmiller/public_html/math/papers/godnumber_rubikscubes_polymathjr2024_v08.pdf) is the maximum number of moves required to solve a Rubik's cube from any scrambled state under a given metric. There is a kind of WCA match that requires competitors to find the fewest-move solution in an hour. I suppose those who enjoy taking math exams will take the challenge, but not me unfortunately.
